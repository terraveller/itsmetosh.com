  • Hey there,

    When I was 15, I went for my first solo trek. Pretty unusual for an Indian teenager. And this was much before 'solo travelling' became a cool thing to boast on social media. Or even before social media became "a thing".

    Now I'm not that old and I don't claim -- to quote my granny -- "having seen more 'Diwalis' than you". But, to my credit, I certainly have seen more Diwalis (Eids, Christmases and new years too) in the wild than you. Because I'm the kind who would grab the slightest opportunity and travel to strange lands. Then bring back tales that would make people back home go like, "Tosh, take me too next time". Since then, I've been taking a lot of people along on various treks, camps and adventure trips personally as well as professionally. Got a bunch of certifications done too to aide my on field experience with theoretical knowledge.

    Genesis of Terraveller lies in one of these camps when I saw the vast demand supply gap. There are far too many takers for adventure tourism but just a few providers and they are all too scattered. Many of them are not even there on the internet. With Terraveller, I want to bridge that gap, serve a plethora of unique travel experiences to travellers and offer discoverability to service providers.

    Feel free to get in touch if what I do excites you too and you would like to discuss anything related to Terraveller or travel in general.
